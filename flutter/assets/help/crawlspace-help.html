01

What is Crawlspace?

Crawlspace is a 3D space roguelike in which you pilot a starship through a procedurally generated galaxy of 250 star systems. The universe is politically complex — a Federation exerts influence across space, alien species compete for territory, and rebel factions operate in the margins.

Your goal: find the mysterious Star One, broadcast its coordinates to the galaxy, and return home before the Federation's agents track you down.

⬡ Tip

Movement is turn-based. Every action you take gives NPC ships a chance to act. Plan ahead — a hostile vessel in the same impulse zone will respond to your moves.

The galaxy just got significantly more dangerous.

Core Loop

Jump between star systems in Hyperspace. Navigate within systems at the System level. Enter close-quarters Impulse combat near planets and stations. Trade, repair, and resupply at inhabited worlds. Avoid the Federation's heat.

02

The Three Space Domains

Space is divided into three nested layers. Your ship's engines determine which layers you can access. The grid view changes to reflect your current domain.

Hyperspace
The galactic layer. Jump between star systems using your hyperdrive. Each system is a node in the network — you can only jump to directly linked systems.
ENTER: H from a star cell
System Level
The tactical system map. An 8×8×8 3D grid containing stars, planets, asteroid fields, and nebulae. Navigate using your sublight drive.
ENTER: . from impulse
Impulse
Close-range combat zone. Generated from the sector cell you occupy. Hazards from that cell carry through. Ships from the same sector also enter.
ENTER: , from system level
⬡ 3D Navigation

All domains are 3D grids. Use Z + arrow to move up one Z-plane and Shift + arrow to move down. The grid displays all Z-depths stacked with perspective — closer cells appear larger and brighter.

05

Hazards

Hazardous cells affect your ship each turn you spend inside them. Hazard intensity varies — a partial nebula will impair scanning, a dense ion storm can knock out systems. Multiple hazards combine into special symbols.

~
Nebula
Blocks scanning in and out of affected cells. Ships inside cannot be detected, and cannot detect others. Safe to fly through.
*
Asteroid Field
Deals hull damage per turn. Heavier fields deal more damage. Energy scooping is possible near dense roid fields.
↯
Ion Storm
Disrupts ship electronics. Can disable systems. At the impulse level, ion storms evolve dynamically (Hodgepodge automaton).
γ
Gamma Radiation
Biological damage to crew. Cumulative exposure increases risk per turn spent in the zone.
⌇
Warp Wake
Edge boundary of an impulse zone. Exiting through the wake returns you to system level.
⚠ Warning

NPC ships are also affected by hazards and will attempt to avoid them. Use hazard zones tactically — a nebula can break scanner lock and let you escape pursuit.

06

Factions & Species

The galaxy is home to multiple species, each with their own homeworld, technology level, and faction alignments. NPC ships display their species' glyph in their faction's color. Hostile factions will engage you on sight if you're in their territory.

The Federation exerts gravitational influence across the galaxy. Core systems (near Mentos, the Federation homeworld) have heavy patrols and strict law enforcement. Frontier systems are lawless. Your heat level determines how aggressively agents pursue you.

07

Your Ship

Your ship has three vital stats displayed in the status panel on the right side of the screen:

Hull
72%
Shields
45%
Energy
88%

Hull — structural integrity. At zero your ship is destroyed and the game ends. Repair at planets.

Shields — absorb incoming damage before hull takes hits. Recharge automatically each turn based on shield system rating.

Energy — powers all systems. Generated by your power plant; consumed by engines, weapons, shields, and active systems. Recharge by scooping a star.

Ship Systems

Your ship has slots for installed systems. Each slot type accepts specific equipment:

Engine (Impulse)
Enables movement at the Impulse domain
Engine (Sublight)
Enables movement at the System domain
Engine (Hyperdrive)
Enables jumps between star systems
Power Generator
Produces energy per tick; determines recharge rate
Shield
Absorbs incoming damage; recharges from energy
Weapon
Direct-fire weapon with cooldown and range profile
Launcher
Ammo-based weapon; requires matching ammo in hold
⬡ Mass Limit

Every system and item has mass. Your ship class determines maximum mass capacity. Running out of mass means you can't add more equipment or ammo. Jettison scrap with J to free up space.

Land on a planet with L when adjacent to it. Planets with shops display available wares; shipyards allow you to buy or sell ships. You can repair hull and recharge systems for credits.

09

Victory & Scoring

Your mission: locate Star One, broadcast its coordinates across the galaxy, then return to the Federation homeworld Mentos. Arriving without broadcasting first leads to immediate execution.

⚠ Victory Condition

You must broadcast the Star One signal before returning home, and the broadcast must reach enough systems to trigger a political collapse. Returning early or without enough broadcasts results in a bad ending.
